One big hindrance is being a 'doormat' or allowing others to 'intimidate' us.

Jo Anderson's definition of a doormat from the "Renewing Love" course:

"They allow one or more persons to dominate them verbally, emotionally, spiritually or physically. Lacking personal worth, they feel they feel they must allow such treatment, not realizing their value in God's sight they oftern accept the abuse because of feelings of guilt over past sins in their life that have not been dealt with and it is as though they need [unishment to atone for their sins. There is a fear underlying their behaviour which keeps them from standing up to a particular individual. Feeling suppressed by this individual, they will often retaliate subconsciously by dominating some other person in their life. If they accept physical abuse they most likely dwell upon self destruction when the going gets rough. They obey with rotten attitudes out of fear in their heart, meanwhile hating themselves all the more every time they allow themselves to behave in this manner."

Jo's definition of intimidation:

"it is a state of mind in which I allow another person's value, opinions, prejudices, ideas, actions, lack of action and personality make me feel defensive. I may even feel unworthy or unknowledgable - even frightened and silences to the point of shame. Intimidating experiences can also leave me feeling put down, insulted, misquoted, misrepresented, demoralized, bought-off, threatened and cheated where a sense of impotency seems overwhelming and unsolvable. Oftern, I may feel a terrible sense of inadequancy and I am unable to cope with another's wit, intelligence, talent, dogmatic or overbearing ways, foolishness, rebellion, anger, abuse, lashing out, ethics, logic, assumptions, or another's expectations. Our intimidation is usually typified by feelings of "I should have said or done this" or "next time I will say or do the following." One is left with little creativity, productivity or self-worth. Great anger and resentment can well up in us if we do not deal with these frustrations.""

"An inner vow is a determination set by the mind and heart into all the being in early life. Vows we make now also affect us, but an inner vow is one set into us as children, ussually forgotten. Our inner being persistently retains such programming no matter what changes of mind and heart may later come about. The clear mark of an inner vow is that it resists the normal growing up process. We do not grow out of them as we do most childish things."

They come into effect at the due time or season usually later on in life when right person or situation may trigger them off. The good as well as the bad vows need to be brought to the Lord and dealt with, so that we are not hindered by the programming of our flesh, but by the Spirit of God."

There is always bitterness, fear or ill feelings or anger behind an inner vow and / or of wanting to be in control.
