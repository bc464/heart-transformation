"It's not what you eat, but what's eating you!"

"Where there is bitterness, God's love cannot be. I will be hindering the spirit of God form flowing through me. It will bind the other person, as well as myself, as well as effect my relationship with God" (quote from Jo Anderson).

I can choose to hold on to an offense, or I can choose to forgive. As i keep my eyes on Jesus, and focus on how He has forgiven me through grace then in the light of His forgiveness of me and through His strength, I can then forgive and let go! And then bless the person.

"God never demands anything from us unless He gives it at the same time."

Are our roots going into bitterness or into God's love? Wherever our roots are - that is where we will get our nourishment.

9 STEPS TO SELF_DESTRUCTION

OUTWARD SIGNS

  1. Deeply wounded
  2. Breakdown of communication between parents / child, husband / wife and friends
  3. Unthankfulness
  4. Stubbornness
  5. Rebellion
  6. Wrong friendships
  7. Justifying that which is wrong
  8. Judge others
  9. Extreme moods

INNER EXPERIENCE

  1. Reject God's voice to forgive - behind every wounded spirit there are two sins:

    Unloving and unforgiving.

  2. Wounded spirit with bitterness as result
  3. Self-pity, reject all love with suspicion
  4. Reject authority
  5. Putting yourself as authority
  6. Acceptance of rebellion
  7. Satisfying impulsive desires
  8. Deep guilt feeling
  9. Thoughts of self-destruction or murder
