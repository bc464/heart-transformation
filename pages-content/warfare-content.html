Rom 6 - What shall we say, then? Shall we go on sinning so that grace may increase? By no means! We are those who have died to sin; how can we live in it any longer? Or don’t you know that all of us who were baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into his death? We were therefore buried with him through baptism into death in order that, just as Christ was raised from the dead through the glory of the Father, we too may live a new life. For if we have been united with him in a death like his, we will certainly also be united with him in a resurrection like his. For we know that our old self was crucified with him so that the body ruled by sin might be done away with, that we should no longer be slaves to sin — because anyone who has died has been set free from sin. Now if we died with Christ, we believe that we will also live with him. For we know that since Christ was raised from the dead, he cannot die again; death no longer has mastery over him. The death he died, he died to sin once for all; but the life he lives, he lives to God. In the same way, count yourselves dead to sin but alive to God in Christ Jesus. Therefore do not let sin reign in your mortal body so that you obey its evil desires. Do not offer any part of yourself to sin as an instrument of wickedness, but rather offer yourselves to God as those who have been brought from death to life; and offer every part of yourself to him as an instrument of righteousness. For sin shall no longer be your master, because you are not under the law, but under grace. Slaves to Righteousness What then? Shall we sin because we are not under the law but under grace? By no means! Don’t you know that when you offer yourselves to someone as obedient slaves, you are slaves of the one you obey —whether you are slaves to sin, which leads to death, or to obedience, which leads to righteousness? But thanks be to God that, though you used to be slaves to sin, you have come to obey from your heart the pattern of teaching that has now claimed your allegiance. You have been set free from sin and have become slaves to righteousness. I am using an example from everyday life because of your human limitations. Just as you used to offer yourselves as slaves to impurity and to ever-increasing wickedness, so now offer yourselves as slaves to righteousness leading to holiness. When you were slaves to sin, you were free from the control of righteousness. What benefit did you reap at that time from the things you are now ashamed of? Those things result in death! But now that you have been set free from sin and have become slaves of God, the benefit you reap leads to holiness, and the result is eternal life. For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord.

UNDERSTANDING THE SHOFAR

by Joy Gilbert (JOY Magazine, October '99)

SHOFAR is the Hebrew word that comes from a root meaning beauty. Through tradition, the word shofar came to mean almost solely "ram's horn."

Literally, shofar is translated as a "sense of incising.". Incising means to cut or burn into. Obviously the sound of the shofar was more than a mere horn blast to the ancient Hebrews for it to earn a name that signified a cutting or burning into the heart and soul of the people. Still today, most would agree that when you hear the sounds of the shofar, it deeply penetrates both spirit and soul.

When you see the word trumpet in the Bible, it is usually referring to the shofar, the trumpet of God. By studying the Scriptures, we see that it is very important to God. Along with the harp, the shofar is the most spoken of instrument in the Bible. While the harp is used to calm and soothe the spirit and soul, the shofar is constantly used to grab hold of the heart of the people. The harp is a consoler whole the shofar is a preparer. It's no wonder that more and more churches are blowing the shofar as we prepare ourselves for the return of our Lord and Saviour.

It frequently was made from a ram's horn; however, any kosher animal horn was used except the cow or ox. Hebrew law prohibits the use of the cow or ox becuase they were used as the image of Baal.

The shofar continues to be made by the same method utilized for over 5000 years and is an object of reat spiritual significance.

The shofar is a symbol of power. The horn is an animal's source of power, its physical strength, what it gores with in order to cause as much damage as possible to its "enemy". The horn equals strength and power. It also represents the ram caught in the thicket when Abraham was about to sacrifice Isaac. Today, it represents God's humble substitutional sacrifice for us, Jesus. It is not considered a musical instrument. According to Jewish law, it is acceptable for both men and women to blow it.

The most popular shofar in the church is the Yemenite triple twist horn from the African Kudu. Animal horns are made of cartilage (fleshy bone), blood, and keratinous material (like fingernails). Removing the cartilage, drilling a hole in the tip for the breath to flow through and smoothing the rough outer edges makes the instrument. It is also heated and pressed to bend its shape for the best use in the hands of a master.

In this it is also symbolic of man. We, too, must remove the flesh from our lives, be pressed by the Holy Spirit (the breath of God), be heated, pressured and bent into obedience, and rid of our rough edges in order to be an effective instrument of God, the ulitmate Master.

When you first buy a Shofar, it has a terrible odor because of a residue of flesh remaining within it and must be cleaned out in order to get rid of the rotting smell. Again, you can see the relevance to man. When we are reborn, there is still that flesh nature in us which must be removed before we can become a sweet fragrance to one another.

Placing small rocks mixed with rock salt into the horn and then shaking it until the dregs of flesh are removed cleans the shofar. This must be repeated from time to time until the odor no longer returns. We, too, are periodically shaken to remove our lingering vestiges of flesh. Like the rocks, the hardness of trials and tribulations purifies us. As for the salt, we are told in Mark 9:49 that "every sacrifice will be seasoned with salt." It brings to mind the sacrifice of our flesh referred to in Galatians 5:24.

In the Jewish tradition, there are different notes or types of noise sounded with the shofar. The first is the "teki'ah". This sound is one continious burst, consisting of one or two tones. It's the sound of triumph, joy and happiness. It was often used to announce the coming if the king. The second noise is the "shevarim". It consist of three shorter blasts of two tones each. This represents a broken, moaning and crying sound, signifying that we are broken before the Lord. The third sound is the "teruah". It's a set of nine or more shorter piercing cries, a plea for mercy. The latter two sound speak powerfully to the intercessor.

More and more Christians are recognising that we are living in a time when the shofar needs to be sounded...

Many times the sounds of happiness and moaning are combined. Why are there both sounds of joy and of sorrow blown from the shofar? There's a story about a man who had a ring made that was engraved with, "this too, shall pass." If he was troubled and in pain, he would look at his ring and remember that the suffering would eventually end. During times of happiness and comfort, he would gaze at his ring and realize that his wealth and good firtune could change for the worse in an instant. The Lord's heart on Tithing and Covenant
By Tertius Ehlers

During a fast, the Lord led me to read Malachi 3:6-12. I sensed and urgency from Him for me to do so. Based on what follows below, I understand why. He has opened certain parts of this Scripture to me over a three day period on a level I have never experienced before, and I knew this is not a message only for me, but also for His body. It is a message form the Lord's heart and therefore important for us to hear it. As lead by the Holy Spirit I jave summarized below the essence of His revelation to me and I do this in the absolute reverential and obedient fear of the Lord.

The Lord revealed that in v6 and 7 He is confirming His covenant with us where He says "For I am the Lord, I do not change; that is why you, O sons of Jacob, are not consumed." And although He is a covenant kepping God and does not change, we however as His people have since our forefathers not obeyed Him, v7 "even from the days of your fathers you have turned aside from My ordinances and have not kept them". The Lord then says if we return to Him, He will return to us. (Is our God's mercy not amazing, awesome and beyond our understanding, since for generations of not keeping His ordinances, He is always ready to return to us if we return to Him.)

The crux of the Lord's revelation, which is the message that He wants us hear and receive, is the answer to the question at the end of v7 "How shall we return" to Him. What must we do and what is acceptable to Him in us returning to Him?

At this point, I remember saying to the Lord "surely Lord the answer to this question must be, that if You say in v7 we have not kept your ordinances, then to return to You we should keep all Your ordinances?" The Lord was quiet for a long time after i said this, and I knwe then I had it wrong, but I sensed the Lord wanted me to wait for the answer, for when it comes, it will be important. And it is!!

Then the Lord provided the answer: Bring all the tithes (the whole tenth of our income) into the storehouse". (v9)

I immediately asked "Lord, how does "bringing all our tithes into the storehouse" compare with "all of Your ordinances not being kept"? How is doing this one thing removing the transgressions of all Your ordinances?" And the Lord answered by saying "If you bring all the tithes (the whole tenth of your income) into My storehouse, the covenant is restored". At this point the anointing was so heavy I could almost not breathe. I managed to ask the Lord "How is it possible that by tithing, the covenant is restored?" and He answered "When you tithe, by the physical act of bringing your title (the whole tenth of your income) into My storehouse, you recognize, accept and acknowledge who I AM, and all that I represent, that all you have is Mine and all I have is yours, and as you give to Me so will I give to you, and even more so - this is the principle of my covenant".

The Lord then paused for a while since I needed to recover from this revelation and to let it sink in.

Then the Lord went bakc to v8 where He says that we are robbing and defrauding Him when we do not bring the whole tenth of our income into His storehouse. He led me to focus on these two words, "rob" and "defraud". He revealed to me that He has not used the words "steal" or "take" from Him, but "rob" and "defraud". The reason why He used the word "rob" is that it means "to unlawfully and intentionally take property belonging to another with violence, the intention to permanently deprive the owner of that property". The Lord revealed to me that He sees not bringing all our tithes (the whole tenth of our income) into His storehouse as an act of violence against Him and as a result that the covenant is broken by this in a violent manner. Our tithes (the whole tenth of our income) is the property of the Lord, it does not beling to us, and by not bringing it into His storehouse we are unlawfully and intentionally with violence depriving Him of that property of which He is the owner and therefore we are "robbing" Him.

By defrauding the Lord we are "unlawfully and intentionally making a misrepresntation which causes actual predujice" to Him. A "misrepresenation" is where an incomplete statement of a material fact is made. The Lord revealed to me that in v10 He has put emphasis on us bringing all our tithes and the whole tenth of our income into His storehouse. Anything less He considers being a misrepresentation of our income and therefore we are defrauding Him. A misrepresentation include where one may use part of our tithe for other causes that is not the Lord's storehouse.

The storehouse is generally seen as the place form which you get your spiritual food. Those that belong to a church would need to tithe to their local church. Those who do not belong to a church would need to ask where they get their spiritual food as that would be their storehouse.

By not bringing the whole tenth of our income into His storehouse, we are not only robbing and defraudung the Lord, but we also robbing the whole nations (v9), since it is from the Lord's storehouse that the tithes are distributed and used by Him for all His work in the nation(s). I experienced that the Lord was quite serious about this, that His work in the nation(s) is held back because His storehouse is empty.

The Lord has revealed to me that it is not He who is cursing those who have robbed Him, but it is the curse that applies to those who are not in covenant with Him. By robbing and defrauding the Lord, the covenant is broken, and as a result one then falls outside of the covenant and therefore comes under this curse (v9).

And then I experienced the beautiful and awesome heart of our covenant-keeping God where He so eagerly waits to bless us once we do bring the whole tenth of our income into His storehouse. Again the Lord's mathematics bowls one over, because by the one act of tithing He promises three blessings, and the total of these blessings far, far outweighs the total of our tithing. This is how the covenant operates, what He has is ours and what we have is His, BUT He has so much, much more than what we have or can give to Him. We can never out give Him!! Listen to the beautiful and amiazing blessings, let it sink in, the Lord wants us to hear and receive this, namely

  • He will open the windows of heaven for us and pour out a blessing that there shall not be room enough to receive it (v10)
  • He will rebuke the enemy from stealing or to destroy us (v11)
  • We shall be a land of delight (which refers to our lives, our family, workplace, income, ralationships etc.)(v12)

The Lord is serious that we can prove Him in this (v10), namely to bring our whole tenth into His storehouse and to prove Him that He will pour out these blessings over is as He promises. Nothing the Lord ex[ects from us He has either not done Himself or is not part of His character. In Deut 8:2(Remember how the LORD your God led you all the way in the wilderness these forty years, to humble and test you in order to know what was in your heart, whether or not you would keep his commands.) the Lord took the Isrealites out of Egypt into the desert to humble them, to prove them, to see what is in their hearts and minds and to determine whether they will obey His commandments. In the same way He has proved us, or is still proving us, He says in v10 that we now have the opportunity to prove Him. He invite us to do so!

We serve an awesome God who does not change. Let us therefore receive the Lord's revelation on this Scripture and return to Him by bringing all our tithes, the whole tenth of our income, into His storehouse that He will return to us and in this way that the covenant then be fully restored.
