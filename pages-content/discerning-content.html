And let everyone who is thirsty [who is painfully conscious of his need of those things by which the soul is refreshed, supported and strengthened]; and whoever [earnestly] desires to do it, let him come, take, appropiate, and drink the water of Life without cost." Revelation 22:17

HOW TO DISCERN THE WILL OF GOD IN YOUR LIFE

You will have peace in your heart, and there will be no uncertainty. "When in doubt, cut it out!"

Ex. 33:14-15 - The LORD replied, “My Presence will go with you, and I will give you rest.” Then Moses said to him, “If your Presence does not go with us, do not send us up from here.

7 IMPORTANT FACTORS TO CONSIDER IN IMPORTANT DECISIONS:

  1. Desire.

    Ps. 37:4 - "Delight yourself also in the Lord, and He will give you the desires and secret petitions of your heart."

  2. Confirmation by Rhema word

    Ask God for a confirmation by two or three others, who don't know anything about it.

  3. Supernatural revelation

    Prophecy, tongues, interpretation of tongues, dreams, visions. These will be a confirmation of what the Lord has already given you. Ask God to confirm through two or three others, who don't know anything about it.

  4. Spiritual leaders

    Share what you have received with spiritual leaders. Does it witness with them and get them to pray with you?

  5. Common sense and wisdom.
  6. Circumstances coming in line.

    Things can suddenly fall in place, sometimes even only at the last minute, so that the Lord can teach you to walk by faith, and not by sight.

  7. Peace.

    You know that, the peach that passes all understanding.

    Phil 4:7 - And the peace of God, which transcends all understanding, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus.

    1 Thess. 5:21 - but test them all; hold on to what is good,

THE BRIDE OF CHRIST

The woman of Proverbs 31 can only be possible in Christ Jesus

Prov. 31:10 - A wife of noble character who can find? She is worth far more than rubies.

Prov. 12:4 - A wife of noble character is her husband’s crown, but a disgraceful wife is like decay in his bones.

1 Cor. 11:7 - A man ought not to cover his head, since he is the image and glory of God; but woman is the glory of man.

Prov. 18:22 - He who finds a wife finds what is good and receives favor from the LORD.

Gen. 1:26-27 - Then God said, “Let us make mankind in our image, in our likeness, so that they may rule over the fish in the sea and the birds in the sky, over the livestock and all the wild animals, and over all the creatures that move along the ground.” So God created mankind in his own image, in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them.

Heb. 1:2 - but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things, and through whom also he made the universe.

Prov. 19:14 - Houses and wealth are inherited from parents, but a prudent wife is from the LORD.

Ps. 104:30 - When you send your Spirit, they are created, and you renew the face of the ground.

Col. 3:9-10 - Do not lie to each other, since you have taken off your old self with its practices and have put on the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge in the image of its Creator.

James 3:8-9 - but no human being can tame the tongue. It is a restless evil, full of deadly poison. With the tongue we praise our Lord and Father, and with it we curse human beings, who have been made in God’s likeness.

Prov. 31:11(Her husband has full confidence in her and lacks nothing of value.) - Trust in the Lord's protection for every circumstance

Prov. 31:12(She brings him good, not harm, all the days of her life.) - Do you do your spouse only good?

Prov. 31:13(She selects wool and flax and works with eager hands.) - The spiritual meaning of wool = striving and of flax = righteousness. You need to flow and mixture of both, as well as being led by the Holy Spirit

Prov. 31:14(She is like the merchant ships, bringing her food from afar.) - She is like the merchant ships, bringing her family food as well as spiritual nourishment

Prov. 31:15(She gets up while it is still night; she provides food for her family and portions for her female servants.) - She is filled with the spiritual food of His Word. She rises while it is still night to study His Word. The maids are angels sent by God to support, protect and help us. Job 23:12 - "I have not departed from the commands of his lips; I have treasured the words of his mouth more than my daily bread." Song of Solomon 8:12 - "But my own vineyard is mine to give; the thousand shekels are for you, Solomon, and two hundred are for those who tend its fruit."

Prov. 31:16(She considers a field and buys it; out of her earnings she plants a vineyard.) - God directs her where to work. "Where You want to use me, there it will be fruitful.

Prov. 31:17(She sets about her work vigorously; her arms are strong for her tasks.) - She is ready for spiritual warfar, psycholigically and physically fit and ready. Ps18

Prov. 31:18(She sees that her trading is profitable, and her lamp does not go out at night.) - Night represents problems, fustration, sorrow, depression, fear, doubt and distrust. Do you keep your "lamp" burning through these times? Night and darkness is where there is an absence of light.

Prov. 31:19(In her hand she holds the distaff and grasps the spindle with her fingers.) - Spindle - She is righteous, led by the Holy Spirit and her salvation is in the fear of the Lord.

Prov. 31:20-21(She opens her arms to the poor and extends her hands to the needy. When it snows, she has no fear for her household; for all of them are clothed in scarlet.) - Scarlet refers to being covered by the blood of Jesus. Snow - lack of warmth and love. She does not fear death, or the cold. Prov. 31:22(She makes coverings for her bed; she is clothed in fine linen and purple.) - Rev 3:5 -"The one who is victorious will, like them, be dressed in white. I will never blot out the name of that person from the book of life, but will acknowledge that name before my Father and his angels." 1 Tim 2:9 - "I also want the women to dress modestly, with decency and propriety, adorning themselves, not with elaborate hairstyles or gold or pearls or expensive clothes, but with good deeds, appropriate for women who profess to worship God." It is unfortunate that this description of God's ideal woman is ussually confined in readers' minds merely to its literal sense - her ablility as a homemaker, as in the picture of Martha of Bethany in Luke 10:38-42 - "As Jesus and his disciples were on their way, he came to a village where a woman named Martha opened her home to him. She had a sister called Mary, who sat at the Lord’s feet listening to what he said. But Martha was distracted by all the preparations that had to be made. She came to him and asked, “Lord, don’t you care that my sister has left me to do the work by myself? Tell her to help me!” “Martha, Martha,” the Lord answered, “you are worried and upset about many things, but few things are needed—or indeed only one. Mary has chosen what is better, and it will not be taken away from her.”. But it is obvious that far more than that is meant. When the summary of what makes her value 'far above rubies' is given (in Prov. 31:30), it is her spiritual life only that is mentioned. One can almost hear the voice of Jesus saying, 'Mary has chosen the good portion...which shall not be taken away from her'. 'Many daughters have done...nobly and well...but you excel them all.' What a glowing description here recorded of this woman in private life, this 'capable, intelligent, and virtuous woman' of Prov. 31! It means she has done more than Mariam, the one who led a nation's women in praise to God(Exod. 15:20,12 - "Then Miriam the prophet, Aaron’s sister, took a timbrel in her hand, and all the women followed her, with timbrels and dancing.
