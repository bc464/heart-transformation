Rom 13:1-5 - Let everyone be subject to the governing authorities, for there is no authority except that which God has established. The authorities that exist have been established by God. Consequently, whoever rebels against the authority is rebelling against what God has instituted, and those who do so will bring judgment on themselves. For rulers hold no terror for those who do right, but for those who do wrong. Do you want to be free from fear of the one in authority? Then do what is right and you will be commended. And when the Chief Shepherd appears, you will receive the crown of glory that will never fade away. In the same way, you who are younger, submit yourselves to your elders. All of you, clothe yourselves with humility toward one another, because, “God opposes the proud but shows favor to the humble.”

"It is so easy to be cowardly - sometimes a very docile person may use the covering of saying she is submissive but really what she is doing is copping out when she ought to speak up as she ought to, to her husband - to be submissive does not mean that we do not speak up and give our opinion or never state what is important to have - this is a misconception we have." (Jo Anderson)

Bill Gothard brings out in the Advanced Basic Youth Conflicts semiar the way one is to appeal to authority. We will adapt this to a wife approaching her husband when he asks unreasonable things of her, or to any authorities, e.g. (Boss at work or spiritual leaders, parents).

  1. They must have a genuine love and respect for him.
  2. They must have taken care of their bitterness.
  3. They must come with a learner;s heart.
  4. They must be loyal to him, trustworthy.
  5. They must appeal to the good in him such as "I know you want the best for me and our family, therefore... Not from the posistion 'You are so demanding so hateful, or unreasonable.'"
  6. They must come with accurate information, accurate with the facts. (Too often women make whole stories around a few points and jump to false conslusions).
  7. Be careful of the wording. Do not use language that blames, accuses or projects failure - all of which threatens him and his position.
  8. Then lastly, they must trust God to work through the authority figure, accepting what God will do in him and through him. Many times, time is needed to let the new idea 'sink' in or for him to change his mind and still maintain dignity.

A husband, or an authority figure, as the authority, has radar built in to catch attitudes. There is a real need for us to know how pure our own heart is, before we point the finger at him. If our attitude is not correct, we must stop at step one, until we ourselves are right with God before expecting more out of him than we do ourselves. God is a God of Wisdom and Creativity. He alone will give common sense and good judgement. When a wife will approach her husband in love and attentiveness, then God will bring out options that neither party had thought about. Give God an opportunity to work in your family. One lade said, "Submission is yielded actions coupled with complete individuality."

TIPS FOR THE WOMAN MARRIED TO THE UNBELIEVER - (Jo Anderson)

  1. Don't think of him as a less spiritual person than yourself.
  2. Don't think God only speaks through you.
  3. Treat him as the godly man he could become.
  4. Seek his counsel; don't always know all the answers.
  5. Admire him exceedingly.
  6. Trust him and allow God to deal with his heart.
  7. His accountability is to God not to you.
  8. Step aside from condemning or protecting your husband so God can deal with him directly.
  9. Give gim the freedom to make a mistake.
