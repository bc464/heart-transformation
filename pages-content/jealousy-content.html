Jealousy

For wherever there is jealousy (envy) and contention (rivalry and selfish ambition), there will also be confucion (unrest, disharmony, rebellion) and all sorts of evil and vile practices. James 3:16-17

BRINGS DIVISION AND DISUNITY

"We are only as wise as we are holy


"These are those who are pure in their own eyes." Prov. 30:12

The dictionary definition: "suspiciously, careful or watchful, or fear that the good which one desires to gain or keep for oneself has been or may be diverted to another; resentful towards another on account of known or suspected rivalry, anger, wrath, indignation, mistrust, doubt."

Jealousy leads to hate - murder, severed relationships, loss of love and trust. When we are jeolous, self-pity comes over us, fears, stubbornness, suspiciousness and we plot evil. Jealousy is a fruit of bitterness. The antidote to jealousy is forgiveness and love.

WHY DO WE GET JEALOUS

Is it because someone is getting something that we want - is it pride?

We can get jeolous of all sorts of things, such as: Possessions, home, children, talents, natural gifts, abilities, friendships, looks, clothes, personality, ministry, their parents, education, denomination, appearance of a person, etc.

Most often the door to a spirit of jealousy is opened during childhood.

TO OVERCOME JEALOUSY STUDY

1 Cor. 12:12-28 - Just as a body, though one, has many parts, but all its many parts form one body, so it is with Christ. For we were all baptized by one Spirit so as to form one body—whether Jews or Gentiles, slave or free —and we were all given the one Spirit to drink. Even so the body is not made up of one part but of many. Now if the foot should say, “Because I am not a hand, I do not belong to the body,” it would not for that reason stop being part of the body. And if the ear should say, “Because I am not an eye, I do not belong to the body,” it would not for that reason stop being part of the body. If the whole body were an eye, where would the sense of hearing be? If the whole body were an ear, where would the sense of smell be? But in fact God has placed the parts in the body, every one of them, just as he wanted them to be. If they were all one part, where would the body be? As it is, there are many parts, but one body. The eye cannot say to the hand, “I don’t need you!” And the head cannot say to the feet, “I don’t need you!” On the contrary, those parts of the body that seem to be weaker are indispensable, and the parts that we think are less honorable we treat with special honor. And the parts that are unpresentable are treated with special modesty, while our presentable parts need no special treatment. But God has put the body together, giving greater honor to the parts that lacked it, so that there should be no division in the body, but that its parts should have equal concern for each other. If one part suffers, every part suffers with it; if one part is honored, every part rejoices with it. Now you are the body of Christ, and each one of you is a part of it. And God has placed in the church first of all apostles, second prophets, third teachers, then miracles, then gifts of healing, of helping, of guidance, and of different kinds of tongues.

We are in a covenant relationship with each other. We are one in spirit - what is mine is alos yours and visa versa. (Note v.26). We cannot say it is 'mine' but 'ours' because we are one in Spirit. No one is more important before the Lord - we have different functions, but not importance, and we need each other.

The awesome accountability is to God.

All of the above scripture show that "God is no respecter of persons: He loves us all the same - "God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son and whosoever believes in Him shall not die but have everlasting life." - John 3:16

As we have a passion to see God being glorified and His Kingdom being extended - we will take our eyes off the person or 'vessel' and put them onto God then there will be no room for jealousy. We get jealous when we don't see our worth in God's eyes - when we are not fully fulfilling our function within the body and being fully fulfilled.

God is going to ask me one day what did you do with what I gave you, not 'what did you do with what I gave to someone else?.' We will be binding ourselves as well as the other person when we are jealous - and both will be losers and God's work and glory will be hindered.

We are free from jealousy when a brother or sister gets honour and we can rejoice with them knowning we are one with them, so in fact we can 'share' in the honour - but if we grind our teeth because they have something we want; then we are still bound by a spirit of jealousy.

HOW TO GET FREE

Rom 12:10 - Be devoted to one another in love. Honor one another above yourselves.

1 Thess. 5:11 - herefore encourage one another and build each other up, just as in fact you are doing.

Be honest and name it before God and ask Him to show it to you, and to see it as He sees it.

  1. Ask the Lord where you opened your heart to a spirit of jealousy (most likely during your childhood, either through feeling that you are the less favoured child, or competing for the attention of parents,etc.).
  2. To confess jealousy and bitterness as sin (the root cause is pride) and bring it under the blood of Jesus.
  3. To forgive.
  4. Break the power of jealousy over your life and command it to go in Jesus' Name, then receive healing and love and let the Balm of Gilead heal the wounds.
  5. To bless the person you have been jealous of and release God's love over him.
  6. As the Lord leads, to confess to the person that you have been jealous of, and ask him to forgive you, and that you have released him.

When you feel good in your heart towards each person that you have forgiven - then you can know you have been released of jealousy and bitterness.

Love is the bond of perfectness.

1 Cor 13:13 - And now these three remain: faith, hope and love. But the greatest of these is love.

Col 3:12-14 - Therefore, as God’s chosen people, holy and dearly loved, clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience. Bear with each other and forgive one another if any of you has a grievance against someone. Forgive as the Lord forgave you. And over all these virtues put on love, which binds them all together in perfect unity.

Intercede for them regularly - take steps to promote their ministry with humility, love and intercession. We can only be hurt to the degree that we love.
